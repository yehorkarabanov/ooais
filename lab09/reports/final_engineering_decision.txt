FINAL ENGINEERING DECISION
==========================

Selected model:
ResNet18 with fine-tuning (fine-tuned last block + classifier head).

Technical justification (based on Lab 8 + Lab 9):
- Classification accuracy: this model achieved the best result (1.0000 in the fine-tuning report), while frozen ResNet18 was lower (0.9722 in one run and 0.9556 in another run), and SimpleCNN was 0.8975.
- Training time: SimpleCNN is much faster (0.89 s), but fine-tuned ResNet18 is still in an acceptable range for this prototype (~36.84 s), and gives a large accuracy gain.
- Implementation complexity: higher than SimpleCNN and slightly higher than frozen transfer learning, because we must unfreeze selected layers and tune more parameters.
- Risk of overfitting: fine-tuning can overfit on small datasets, but in our experiments it did not degrade class performance and gave the strongest confusion matrix result.
- Robustness on unknown inputs: like other closed-set classifiers, it can still force known-class predictions on unknown images. Transfer learning alone does not solve OOD detection.
- Suitability for small datasets: strong, because it reuses ImageNet features and adapts them to the Earth Observation classes.
- Expected operational reliability: best among tested options due to highest accuracy and better class separation, plus Grad-CAM evidence that model focus is on meaningful land-cover regions.

Expected advantages:
- Highest measured classification quality.
- Better feature reuse from pretrained representation than training from scratch.
- Good balance between performance and training cost for a prototype system.

Possible risks:
- Overconfidence on unknown/out-of-distribution inputs.
- More complex training pipeline than frozen transfer setup.
- Results may vary between runs (seen in frozen-model metrics), so reproducibility controls are needed.

Deployment considerations:
- Add confidence thresholding + unknown/anomaly detection before operational use.
- Keep preprocessing exactly aligned with pretrained model requirements (normalization, image size).
- Monitor post-deployment drift and periodically re-validate on fresh satellite samples.
- Prefer fine-tuning updates when new labeled data arrives, but keep frozen model as fallback baseline.

Final choice:
For this prototype Earth Observation system, I would deploy ResNet18 with fine-tuning, because it gives the best accuracy and the most reliable class-level behavior while keeping training cost manageable.
